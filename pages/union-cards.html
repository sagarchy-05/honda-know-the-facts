A Union Card Is A Legal Document
The principle to remember is that a Union Authorization Card is a legal document that transfers rights you have today as a Honda associate to a union. A signed card also lengthens the union organizing effort since it is valid for 12 months after it is signed. During the card-signing phase of a campaign, an unsigned card can send a message that you don’t want the union and you want the union’s drive to end. You should take great care and caution in deciding whether to sign, just as you would any other important legal document.
Also, be aware that if the union collects signed cards from 50% of associates, it can automatically become your collective bargaining representative, without an election.
Facts About Union Authorization Cards.
Many workers complain that organizers attempt to pressure or intimidate them during a union campaign. The union supporter will ask a worker to sign a card and not leave the worker alone. Or, the union supporter will even come to their homes to pressure them into signing.

If someone reaches out to you – either in person or online – and asks you to sign, you have the right to say “no,” and the union supporter cannot pressure you. The union supporter must respect your decision.

If someone comes to your door, you do not have to talk to them, or you can tell them that you prefer to keep business separate from family life.

NO ONE CAN PRESSURE YOU INTO SIGNING A UNION CARD.

Be wary of what they may say to get you to sign.
“All the card does is say that you want a secret ballot election on whether to have a union.”

This statement is misleading. Read the card.

Whether an online card or a physical card, be aware that signing it says that you “authorize the UAW to represent me in collective bargaining.”
